Naloxone Opioids Application

Information about Naloxone

Naloxone is a FDA - approved medication commonly referred to as the 'opioid antagonist'. It is used to counteract the effects of opioid overdoses, specifically, life-threatening overdose effects that would otherwise not let an overdose victim breathe correctly. Naloxone is a non-addictive medication that can be administered through the nose and purchased over the counter. Naloxone will only work on people that have opioids in their system and will not have any negative side effects on non-opioid users if administered. It is a temporary drug that wears off in minutes. Naloxone comes in many forms to administer however the most rapid is via nasal administration. We recommend using the nasal form given its rapid ease of administration and simplicity.



Information about Administering Naloxone

1) Remove Naloxone Nasal Spay from packaging. Peel back the sealed tab to open the nasal spray.


2) Hold the Naloxone nasal spray with first and middle fingers on either side of the output nozzle, put your thumb on the bottom of the plunger. Do not press plunger until you are ready to administer.


3) Tilt the person's head back and support their neck with your hand. Insert the tip of the nozzle into one nostril until your fingers positioned on the nozzle are against the bottom of the person's nose.


4. Press the plunger with thumb firmly to give dose of Naloxone nasal spray. Remove the nasal spray from nostril after giving the dose.


Information about Opioids

Opioids are a class of medications that specializes in pain relief. Opioids essentially reduce pain by lessening the intensity of the pain signals reaching the brain. Several different medications fall under this class, some of the more common ones are hydrocodone, oxycodone, morphine, and codeine. These medications are used for an array of different pain scenarios, such as, injury-related pain and pain from post-surgical procedures. No matter what the opioid prescribed is, excessive use of an opioid can be fatal.


LifeKit for Opioid Users

LifeKit is the first and most innovative wearable-integrated, vital monitoring application aimed at helping solve the opioid overdose epidemic. The goal of this application is help opioid users overcome deadly overdoses by monitoring vital signs through wearable technology. LifeKit aims to detect overdoses before fatal incidents happen and save lives by sending alerts to nearby emergency responders, naloxone carriers, family members, and doctors.

To use the application - simply download and register within the application as an opioid user. After registration is complete, a short tutorial and calibration with your integrated wearable device will be required. You're all set to go, simply keep the application running the background and have your wearable device on you during opioid usage.


LifeKit for Naloxone Carriers

LifeKit is a simple, yet life changing application that could help good samaritans become heroes. By carrying naloxone and using this application, users will be alerted of nearby potentially fatal overdose incidents. Users will, then, be able to respond in the nick of time and save overdose victims by administering naloxone and reversing the effects of the overdose almost immediately.

To use the application - simply download and register within the application as a naloxone carrier. After registration is complete, you will be required to watch a short tutorial on naloxone administering. You're all set to go and save lives, simply carry naloxone with you at all times and having the application running in the background.
